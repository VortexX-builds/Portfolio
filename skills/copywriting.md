# Copywriting — Rules and Tone

> The about copy is the law. Every word on this site should be measured against it.

---

## The Benchmark — About Copy

> I'm Sloak Gohil. I build websites that don't look like websites.
>
> React, Three.js, GSAP, TypeScript — the technical side is handled. What actually matters is that every project I touch feels premium, intentional, and nothing like the generic slop flooding the internet.
>
> If you can feel the difference, you already know what I do.

Study this. This is the voice. Match it.

---

## Absolute Rules

### No em dashes. Ever.
Not `—`. Not `--`. Not `&mdash;`. Nothing.
Use a period, a comma, or restructure the sentence.

**Wrong:** "React, Three.js — the technical side is handled."  
**Right:** "React, Three.js. The technical side is handled."

### No developer cliches
The following words and phrases are banned from this site:

- passionate / passion
- creative / creativity / creative solutions
- problem-solver / problem-solving
- innovative / innovation
- dedicated / dedicated developer
- driven
- results-oriented / results-driven
- unique / uniquely qualified
- skill set
- leverage (as a verb)
- synergy, holistic, robust (in any context)
- "I love what I do"
- "with X years of experience"

### No hedging language
Remove: "I try to", "I aim to", "I hope to", "I strive to"  
Replace with declarative statements: "I build", "I ship", "Every project I touch"

---

## Tone Reference

**Voice:** Direct. Confident. Understated.  
**Rhythm:** Short sentences. Occasional fragments for effect.  
**Stance:** The work speaks — the copy does not oversell it.

Read the copy out loud. If it sounds like a LinkedIn bio, rewrite it.

## Punctuation Rules

- Periods end declarative statements. Use them.
- No exclamation marks anywhere.
- No ellipses (...) in body copy.
- Em dashes forbidden (see above).
- Parentheses acceptable sparingly for technical asides only.
- Serial comma (Oxford comma) is used.
